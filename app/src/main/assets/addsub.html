Lesson 1.4: Addition and Subtraction of Decimals

A. Addition of Decimals:

When adding decimals, it's crucial to align the decimal points. The process is similar to adding whole numbers, but you must consider the placement of the decimal point.

Example: Consider adding 12.345 and 67.89.

  1. Align the decimals:

    2. 12.345
    + 67.89

  2. Add the digits as you would with whole numbers:

    3. 80.235

In this example, each digit is added based on its position, and the decimal point is placed in the result at the same position as in the original numbers.

B. Subtraction of Decimals:

Subtracting decimals involves aligning the decimal points and subtracting the digits as you would with whole numbers. Borrowing may be required, similar to subtraction in whole numbers.

Example: Let's subtract 67.89 from 80.235.

  1. Align the decimals:

    2. 80.235
    - 67.89

  2. Subtract the digits as you would with whole numbers:

    3. 12.345

In this example, each digit is subtracted based on its position, and the decimal point is placed in the result at the same position as in the original numbers.

C. Rounding Decimals:

Rounding decimals is the process of approximating a decimal number to a specified place value. When rounding, you identify the desired place value and determine whether the digit in the next place is 5 or greater.

Example: Round 3.678 to the nearest tenth.

  1. Identify the tenths place:

    2. 3.678

  2. Since the digit in the hundredths place (8) is 5 or greater, round up the digit in the tenths place:

    3. 3.7

The rounded value of 3.678 to the nearest tenth is 3.7.

D. Real-World Problem 1:

Let's solve a more complex real-world problem involving the addition of decimal numbers:

Problem: You are buying multiple items at a store with varying quantities and prices:

  • Item 1: 2.5 units at $3.50 per unit
  • Item 2: 1.75 units at $7.20 per unit
  • Item 3: 3.2 units at $2.10 per unit

Calculate the total cost of all items.

  1. Calculate the cost of each item:
    • Item 1: \(2.5 \times \$3.50\)
    • Item 2: \(1.75 \times \$7.20\)
    • Item 3: \(3.2 \times \$2.10\)
  2. Align the decimals and add the total costs:

    3. 7.50
    + 12.60
    + 6.72

  3. The total cost of all items is \$26.82.

E. Real-World Problem 2:

Now, let's solve a more intricate real-world problem involving the subtraction of decimal numbers:

Problem: You have a budget of $100, and you want to track your expenses. Create a ledger of your account with the following transactions:

  • Transaction 1: $150.75
  • Transaction 2: $89.30
  • Transaction 3: $25.50

Calculate the remaining balance after these transactions.

  1. Align the decimals and subtract the transaction amounts:

    2. 150.75
    - 89.30
    - 25.50

  2. The remaining balance is $35.95.
