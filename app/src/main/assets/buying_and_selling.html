Lesson 3.1: Buying and Selling

A. Definition and Examples of Buying and Selling:

Understanding Basic Concepts and Terms:

Learn the basic concepts and terms related to buying and selling:

  • Cost Price: The amount of money spent to produce or purchase an item.
  • Selling Price: The amount at which an item is sold to customers.
  • Profit: The positive difference between the selling price and the cost price.
  • Loss: The negative difference between the selling price and the cost price.
  • Markup: The percentage increase from the cost price to the selling price.
  • Markdown: The percentage decrease from the selling price to the cost price.
  • Discount: A reduction in the selling price of an item.
  • Commission: A percentage of the selling price paid to a salesperson as compensation.

Key Points:

  • Understand how to calculate and apply concepts in various buying and selling scenarios.
  • Interpret profit, loss, markup, markdown, discount, and commission percentages for effective decision-making.
  • Apply learned concepts to solve real-life problems and make informed business decisions.

B. Computation of Profit and Loss:

How to Calculate Profit and Loss:

Learn how to calculate the amount and percentage of profit or loss in buying and selling transactions using formulas and examples.

Formula for Profit: Profit = Selling Price - Cost Price

Formula for Loss: Loss = Cost Price - Selling Price

Formula for Profit Percentage: Profit Percentage = (Profit / Cost Price) * 100

Formula for Loss Percentage: Loss Percentage = (Loss / Cost Price) * 100

Example: If you buy a product for ₱50 and sell it for ₱70, the profit is ₱20.

Profit Percentage: (20 / 50) * 100 = 40%

Example: If you buy a product for ₱80 and sell it for ₱60, the loss is ₱20.

Loss Percentage: (20 / 80) * 100 = 25%

C. Computation of Markup and Markdown:

How to Calculate Markup and Markdown:

Learn how to calculate the amount and percentage of markup or markdown in buying and selling transactions using formulas and examples.

Formula for Markup: Markup = Selling Price - Cost Price

Formula for Markdown: Markdown = Cost Price - Selling Price

Formula for Markup Percentage: Markup Percentage = (Markup / Cost Price) * 100

Formula for Markdown Percentage: Markdown Percentage = (Markdown / Cost Price) * 100

Example: If you buy a product for ₱50 and sell it for ₱70, the markup is ₱20.

Markup Percentage: (20 / 50) * 100 = 40%

Example: If you buy a product for ₱80 and sell it for ₱60, the markdown is ₱20.

Markdown Percentage: (20 / 80) * 100 = 25%

D. Computation of Discount and Commission:

How to Calculate Discount and Commission:

Learn how to calculate the amount and percentage of discount or commission in buying and selling transactions using formulas and examples.

Formula for Discount: Discount = Original Price - Discounted Price

Formula for Commission: Commission = (Commission Percentage / 100) * Selling Price

Formula for Discount Percentage: Discount Percentage = (Discount / Original Price) * 100

Formula for Commission Percentage: Commission Percentage = (Commission / Selling Price) * 100

Example: If a product's original price is ₱100 and it is discounted to ₱80, the discount is ₱20.

Discount Percentage: (20 / 100) * 100 = 20%

Example: If a salesperson receives a 5% commission on a ₱200 sale, the commission is ₱10.

Commission Percentage: (10 / 200) * 100 = 5%

E. Real-life Situations Involving Buying and Selling:

Explore and solve problems related to buying and selling in real-life scenarios:

Example:

A business needs to determine the breakeven point for a product with a production cost of ₱50, a selling price of ₱80, and fixed costs of ₱2,000.

Breakeven Point: Fixed Costs / (Selling Price - Production Cost)

Substitute values: ₱2,000 / (₱80 - ₱50)

Breakeven Point: ₱2,000 / ₱30 = 66.67 units

The business needs to sell approximately 67 units to cover its costs and start making a profit.
