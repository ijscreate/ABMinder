Checking Account Overview

A checking account is a deposit product that comes with a checkbook for funds disbursement, and it may also come with a debit card for accessing funds through ATMs, Point-of-Sale Machines, or for online purchases. Checking accounts are useful for managing transactions, as many companies in the Philippines still require the use of post-dated checks for loan payments, rent, or amortization. Checks provide a sense of security for landlords or condo owners that their tenants are bound by an obligation to fund their checks versus monthly cash payments.

To open a checking account in the Philippines, one needs to personally be at the bank, as the requirements may vary per bank. However, most banks ask for a valid ID, with your photo and clear information, such as a passport, driver's license, or any other legally issued ID. Foreign nationals living in the Philippines are often required to present their Alien Certificate of Registration (ACR) I-Card as well as their passport. For young adults, having a checking account is essential for managing transactions and tracking expenses. It can help make spending practical and safe, especially when dealing with post-dated checks for various transactions. Checking accounts are widely used in the country for practical reasons, and they can be advantageous in many situations.

When choosing a bank to open a checking account, it's best to stay with large, national banks, like the Philippine National Bank or the Bank of the Philippine Islands, as well as international banks like Citibank and HSBC. These banks offer a wide range of services, including internet, ATM, and cellphone banking, and deposits are insured by the Philippine Deposit Insurance Corporation (PDIC). Thrift or rural banks typically offer limited services and could be at risk of closing down with little notice, so it's best to avoid them when learning how to set up a bank account in the Philippines.

Types of Checking Accounts

  • Entry-Level Checking Accounts: Basic accounts ideal for first-time depositors with low initial deposit and maintaining balance requirements.
  • Personal/Individual Checking Accounts: For personal use with checks and ATM/debit card.
  • Business/Corporate Checking Accounts: Designed for SMEs and corporations with higher maintaining balances.
  • All-in-One Checking Accounts: Combine features of savings and checking accounts, usually interest-earning with high initial deposit.

Some examples of checking accounts in the Philippines include the OneAccount High-yield savings and checking in one, Dragon Checking Elite with higher interest rates, and eWoman Checking, tailored for women.

To Open a Checking Account in the Philippines:

Requirements for Local Residents:

  • Valid ID: A valid ID with a photo and clear information is required. Accepted IDs include a passport, driver's license, or any other legally issued ID.
  • Proof of Address: Proof of address, such as a utility bill or residential lease contract, must be presented.
  • Minimum Initial Deposit: A minimum initial deposit is required, which varies by bank.
  • Bank Reference: A bank reference from the country where you're a citizen or a permanent resident may be required.
  • Additional Requirements: Some banks may require additional documents, such as an Alien Certificate of Registration (ACR) I-Card, Immigrant Certification of Registration (ICR), or a written certification from a reference bank.

It's important to note that the requirements may vary per bank, so it's best to inquire or check the bank's website ahead of time. Additionally, it's best to stay with any of the large, national banks, like the Philippine National Bank or the Bank of the Philippine Islands, as well as international banks like Citibank and HSBC, as they offer a wide range of services, including internet, ATM, and cellphone banking, and deposits are insured by the Philippine Deposit Insurance Corporation (PDIC). Thrift or rural banks typically offer limited services and could be at risk of closing down with little notice.

Uses of Checking Accounts

Checking accounts offer easy access to funds for daily transactions, making them useful for various payments such as personal loans, utility bills, rental fees, tuition, and business expenses.
