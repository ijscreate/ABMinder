Gross Profit vs Net Income

Overview

Gross profit and net income are two important financial metrics used to assess the performance of a business. While both are indicators of profitability, they represent different aspects of a company's financial health.

Related Terms

  • Operating Expenses: These are the costs associated with running a company's day-to-day operations, such as rent, utilities, salaries, and marketing expenses.
  • Cost of Goods Sold (COGS): COGS represents the direct costs incurred by a company to produce goods or services that are sold to customers. It includes material costs, labor costs, and overhead expenses directly related to production.
  • Profit Margin: Profit margin is a financial metric that measures the profitability of a company by comparing its net income to its total revenue. It indicates how efficiently a company is generating profits from its operations.
  • EBITDA (Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization): EBITDA is a measure of a company's operating performance and is used to evaluate its profitability without the influence of non-operating expenses, financing costs, or accounting practices.
  • Revenue: Revenue, also known as sales or turnover, is the total amount of money generated by a company from its business activities, such as selling products or services to customers.

Gross Profit

Gross income, also known as gross pay or gross earnings, refers to the total income earned by an individual before any deductions or taxes are subtracted. It represents the total amount of money received from all sources of income, including wages, salaries, bonuses, tips, commissions, rental income, and any other forms of earnings.

Gross income includes both taxable and non-taxable income and is typically stated as an annual figure. It is important to note that gross income does not reflect the amount of money an individual takes home or their net income, as it does not consider deductions such as taxes, insurance premiums, retirement contributions, or other withholdings.

Gross income serves as the starting point for determining an individual's taxable income. It is reported on tax returns and is used as the basis for calculating the amount of income tax owed to the government.

Formula for Businesses:

Gross Income = Total Revenue − Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) \text{Gross Income} = \text{Total Revenue} - \text{Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)}

Formula for Individuals:

Gross Income = Total Income (before taxes and other deductions) \text{Gross Income} = \text{Total Income (before taxes and other deductions)}

For example, John’s job as a marketing executive provides him with a base salary of ₱60,000 per year. This amount is distributed to him in equal monthly payments, resulting in a pre-tax monthly income of ₱5,000 (₱60,000 / 12 months).

In addition to his salary, John receives an annual performance bonus of ₱5,000. This bonus is typically given at the end of the fiscal year to acknowledge his outstanding work and accomplishments.

Moreover, John owns a property that yields rental income. His property, a small apartment, is rented out for ₱1,500 per month, leading to an annual rental income of ₱18,000.

To compute John’s gross income, we sum up all his income sources:

  • Salary: ₱60,000 per year (₱5,000 per month)
  • Bonus: ₱5,000
  • Rental income: ₱18,000

Total gross income = ₱60,000 + ₱5,000 + ₱18,000 = ₱83,000 per year

So, John’s gross income is ₱83,000 annually. This represents the total amount he earns before any taxes or deductions are applied.

It’s important to note that gross income can fluctuate based on an individual’s situation. Other factors that can influence gross income include additional income sources, such as part-time jobs, freelance work, investments, or dividends. Furthermore, elements like overtime pay, commissions, or tips can also add to an individual’s gross income.

Calculating gross income is vital for tax purposes, as it assists in determining an individual’s taxable income and ensures accurate reporting and adherence to tax laws.

Let's consider another example.

Revenue Streams:

  • Bread sales: ₱1,000,000 per year
  • Pastry sales: ₱800,000 per year
  • Catering services: ₱200,000 per year

Cost of Goods Sold (COGS):

  • Ingredients cost: ₱400,000 per year
  • Packaging cost: ₱100,000 per year

To calculate the gross income for Sunshine Bakery, we subtract the COGS from the total revenue:

Total Revenue = Bread sales + Pastry sales + Catering services

Total Revenue = ₱1,000,000 + ₱800,000 + ₱200,000 = ₱2,000,000

COGS = Ingredients cost + Packaging cost

COGS = ₱400,000 + ₱100,000 = ₱500,000

Now, we can calculate the Gross Income:

Gross Income = Total Revenue - COGS

Gross Income = ₱2,000,000 - ₱500,000 = ₱1,500,000

So, the gross income for Sunshine Bakery is ₱1,500,000 per year. This represents the total profit the bakery makes before any other expenses (like rent, salaries, utilities) are deducted. It’s a measure of the bakery’s efficiency in turning raw ingredients into profitable baked goods. Remember, the higher the gross income, the more efficient the business is considered to be.

Net Income

Net income, also known as net earnings or net profit, refers to the amount of money an individual or business has after deducting all applicable expenses from their gross income. It represents the actual income or profit that remains after accounting for various costs, such as taxes, operating expenses, interest payments, and other deductions.

Net income is a crucial financial indicator as it provides insight into the overall profitability of an individual or business. It is often reported on income statements or financial statements and is used to assess the financial performance and profitability of an entity.

For individuals, net income is often referred to as take-home pay or disposable income, as it represents the amount of money available for personal spending, savings, investments, or other financial goals.

For businesses, net income is a key metric for evaluating financial performance, shareholder returns, and determining the overall health and profitability of the organization.

It's important to note that net income may also include other items like non-recurring gains or losses, dividends, and other income streams specific to an individual or business.

Formula for Businesses:

Net Income = Gross Income − Operating Expenses − Interest − Taxes \text{Net Income} = \text{Gross Income} - \text{Operating Expenses} - \text{Interest} - \text{Taxes}

Formula for Individuals:

Net Income = Gross Income − Taxes − Other Deductions \text{Net Income} = \text{Gross Income} - \text{Taxes} - \text{Other Deductions}

Let’s continue with the Sunshine Bakery example for calculating net income:

Gross income:

  • ₱1,500,000 (as calculated previously)

Operating Expenses:

  • Rent: ₱300,000 per year
  • Salaries: ₱500,000 per year
  • Utilities: ₱100,000 per year

Interest:

  • ₱50,000 per year (on business loans)

Taxes:

  • ₱200,000 per year

To calculate the net income for Sunshine Bakery, we subtract the operating expenses, interest, and taxes from the gross income:

Operating Expenses = Rent + Salaries + Utilities
Operating Expenses = ₱300,000 + ₱500,000 + ₱100,000 = ₱900,000

Now, we can calculate the Net Income:

Net Income = Gross Income - Operating Expenses - Interest - Taxes
Net Income = ₱1,500,000 - ₱900,000 - ₱50,000 - ₱200,000 = ₱350,000

So, the net income for Sunshine Bakery is ₱350,000 per year. This represents the total profit the bakery makes after all expenses are deducted. It’s a measure of the bakery’s overall profitability after accounting for all costs. Remember, the higher the net income, the more profitable the business is considered to be.
