Consumer and Organizational Markets

Consumer Markets:

Individuals and households who purchase products and services for personal consumption.

Consumer Buying Process:

  • Problem/Need Recognition
  • Information Search
  • Alternative Evaluation
  • Purchase Decision
  • Post-Purchase Decision

Consumer Buying Roles:

  • Initiator
  • Influencer
  • Decider
  • Buyer
  • User

Consumer Market and Buying Behavior

Marketing Stimuli: Elements of the marketing mix (4 Ps: Product, Price, Place, Promotion).

Consumer Behavior Model:

  • Consumer Cultural Factors:
    • Culture
    • Subculture
    • Social Class
  • Consumer Social Factors:
    • Reference Groups
    • Aspirational Groups
    • Role and Status
  • Consumer Personal Factors:
    • Age & Life Cycle
    • Lifestyle
    • Personality
    • Occupation
  • Consumer Psychological Factors:
    • Motivation
    • Beliefs & Attitude
    • Perception
    • Learning

Marketing Mix (4 Ps)

  • Product: The item or service being sold, satisfying consumer needs.
  • Price: The cost paid by the consumer.
  • Place: The location where the product is available.
  • Promotion: Advertising and communication strategies to reach the target market.

Understanding consumer behavior, the marketing mix, and the factors influencing consumer decisions is crucial for developing effective marketing strategies. By considering cultural, social, personal, and psychological factors, businesses can tailor their products, pricing, distribution, and promotion to meet consumer needs and preferences effectively.
