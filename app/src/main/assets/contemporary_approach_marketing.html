CONTEMPORARY APPROACH TO MARKETING

The focal point of the contemporary approach to marketing is geared towards the customer and their needs with regards to the product and the services. In short, the contemporary approach to marketing is more focused on customers, rather than seeking to pull customers to a product, whatever the cost (traditional approaches). Oftentimes, contemporary marketing approaches use many strategies to build a relationship with their clients.

Difference between Contemporary Marketing Orientation and Traditional Marketing Orientation:

  • The main difference between the contemporary marketing orientation and traditional marketing orientation is that while the former is consumer-focused, the latter is company-focused.
  • Traditional marketing is concerned with pulling customers and does not really consider the customer's diverse needs. Rather, it is concerned with the market or industry in which the company operates.
  • Contemporary marketing aims for customer satisfaction in order to build a relationship. Therefore, the types of marketing strategies under contemporary marketing are concerned with fulfilling the needs of customers.

Contemporary Approaches to Marketing:

  1. Relationship Marketing
    Relationship marketing approach helps businesses build a strong connection with their clients. This, in turn, helps businesses create lifelong bonds with their loyal customers. The need to maintain and establish relationships with customers these days is highly important because it not only helps them understand their audience in the long-run, but also reduces the amount needed to generate new sales compared to traditional marketing approaches.
  2. Industrial Marketing
    Also known as business-to-business marketing (B2B), Industrial Marketing is a branch of marketing that involves one business dealing with another business. Rather than targeting their customers, the businesses are targeting other businesses in this type of marketing. If the industrial buyers are satisfied with their products and services (including price), long-term relations can be created. However, it is good to note that since there are fewer businesses in comparison to consumers in general, the total number of business buyers will be less.
  3. Social Marketing
    Social marketing is a branch of marketing that attempts to bring social change in a community or globally. It combines marketing strategies and theories with other plans to achieve social change. This branch of marketing seeks to benefit individuals and different communities. The goal of social advertising campaigns is to promote ideas that either encourage positive behaviors like caring for the environment or discourage negative behaviors, such as speeding or smoking in public areas. In this way, social marketing “sells” the well-being of society as a whole.

Contemporary Social Marketing Approaches followed by organizations:

  1. Cause Marketing – It is the type of marketing in which a company and charity team up together and spread awareness for a good cause. If the campaign is successful, it can help in increasing the business value for the company.
  2. Event Marketing – It is the type of marketing in which marketers conduct promotional events and promote their products and services in those events. This involves face-to-face contact and helps in increasing the credibility of the brand.
  3. Green Marketing – It is the practice of marketing in which the services and goods are marketed from their environmental benefits. Research has shown that the customers are willing to pay extra for products from companies which are concerned about the environment and committed to sustainable development.

Examples of contemporary marketing strategies include:

  • Content Marketing: Content marketing involves creating and distributing valuable, relevant, and consistent content to attract and retain a clearly defined audience. This content can take various forms such as blog posts, videos, infographics, and podcasts. Content marketing aims to educate, inform, or entertain the audience rather than directly promoting a brand or product.
  • Influencer Marketing: Influencer marketing involves partnering with individuals who have a large and engaged following on social media platforms to promote products or services. Influencers can range from celebrities to industry experts and micro-influencers. Brands collaborate with influencers to leverage their credibility and reach to connect with their target audience authentically.
  • Loyalty Marketing: Loyalty marketing focuses on retaining existing customers and encouraging repeat purchases through rewards programs, special offers, and personalized communication. By rewarding customer loyalty, businesses aim to strengthen the relationship with their customers and increase customer lifetime value.
  • Programmatic Advertising: Programmatic advertising uses algorithms and automated processes to buy and optimize digital ad placements in real-time. This approach allows advertisers to target specific audiences based on demographic, behavioral, and contextual data, optimizing ad delivery for maximum effectiveness and efficiency.
  • Social Media Advertising: Social media advertising involves creating and placing ads on social media platforms such as Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, LinkedIn, and Pinterest. These ads can be targeted based on demographics, interests, behaviors, and other factors, allowing businesses to reach their desired audience with precision.
  • Targeted Email Campaigns: Targeted email campaigns involve sending personalized and relevant emails to segmented lists of subscribers. By tailoring the content and offers to the interests and preferences of each recipient, businesses can increase engagement, conversions, and customer satisfaction.
  • Gamification: Gamification integrates game design elements and mechanics into non-game contexts, such as marketing campaigns, to engage and motivate audiences. By incorporating elements like points, badges, leaderboards, and challenges, businesses can make interactions with their brand more enjoyable and rewarding, driving user engagement and loyalty.
  • Omni-Channel Marketing: Omni-channel marketing provides a seamless and integrated experience across multiple channels and touchpoints, including online and offline channels such as websites, mobile apps, social media, brick-and-mortar stores, and customer service. By ensuring consistency and continuity across channels, businesses can deliver a unified brand experience and meet customer expectations at every stage of the buyer's journey.
  • Global Marketing Strategy: A global marketing strategy involves adapting marketing tactics and messaging to suit diverse international markets while maintaining a consistent brand identity. This approach considers cultural, linguistic, regulatory, and economic differences to effectively reach and resonate with audiences worldwide.
  • Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Big Data: AI and big data technologies enable marketers to collect, analyze, and interpret vast amounts of data to gain actionable insights into consumer behavior, preferences, and trends. By leveraging AI-driven algorithms and machine learning models, businesses can personalize marketing efforts, optimize targeting and messaging, and improve overall campaign performance.

Social media can be used in contemporary marketing strategies in several ways, including:

  • Content Marketing: Sharing blog posts, articles, videos, and infographics on social media platforms to attract and engage audiences.
  • Influencer Marketing: Collaborating with influencers to create sponsored content and promotions that reach their followers on social media.
  • Loyalty Marketing: Engaging with customers through social media channels to provide exclusive offers, rewards, and personalized communication.
  • Programmatic Advertising: Using social media ad platforms to set up automated ad campaigns that target specific audience segments based on their interests, demographics, and behaviors.
  • Social Media Advertising: Running targeted ads on social media platforms to reach and engage with a desired audience, driving traffic, leads, and conversions.
  • Targeted Email Campaigns: Integrating social media data with email marketing platforms to personalize email content and offers based on social media interactions and preferences.
  • Gamification: Creating social media contests, quizzes, challenges, and interactive experiences to gamify brand interactions and encourage user participation and engagement.
  • Omni-Channel Marketing: Providing consistent messaging, branding, and customer service across social media channels, websites, mobile apps, and offline touchpoints to deliver a seamless and cohesive brand experience.
  • Global Marketing Strategy: Tailoring social media content, messaging, and advertising to resonate with culturally diverse audiences in different regions and markets.
