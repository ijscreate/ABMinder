Statement of Financial Position Account Titles

Cash

This refers to the currencies, checks, money orders, bank deposits, and other money equivalents.

Example: ABC Bookstore has Php 20,000 in cash as of December 31, 2023.

Trading account securities

These include stocks of companies listed in the stock exchange and are readily convertible into cash.

Example: ABC Bookstore has Php 15,000 in trading account securities as of December 31, 2023.

Notes receivable

This includes claims of the business from customers and third parties that are evidenced by formal instruments of credit such as promissory notes.

Example: ABC Bookstore has Php 10,000 in notes receivable as of December 31, 2023.

Accounts receivables

This pertains to claims of the business from customers for sales of products or rendering services.

Example: ABC Bookstore has Php 50,000 in accounts receivables as of December 31, 2023.

Allowance for bad debts

This is a contra account against accounts receivables. The net amount is called net realizable value.

Example: ABC Bookstore has an allowance for bad debts of Php 3,000 as of December 31, 2023.

Supplies

These include various office or shop supplies that are necessary in the operation of the business.

Example: ABC Bookstore has Php 5,000 in supplies as of December 31, 2023.

Equipment

This refers to office equipment such as computer sets, printers, air conditioners, and delivery equipment (vehicles).

Example: ABC Bookstore has Php 30,000 in equipment as of December 31, 2023.

Furniture and Fixtures

These pertain to tables, chairs, cabinets, counters, and other types of furniture used in the business.

Example: ABC Bookstore has Php 20,000 in furniture and fixtures as of December 31, 2023.

Accumulated Depreciation

This is a deduction (except for land) from property, plant, and equipment.

Example: ABC Bookstore has Php 5,000 in accumulated depreciation as of December 31, 2023.

Accounts payable

This refers to the debt of the business due to purchases of products or services that are to be paid on the future date.

Example: ABC Bookstore has Php 10,000 in accounts payable as of December 31, 2023.

Notes payable

This covers loans obtained by the business from financial institutions or private individuals and entities supported by promissory notes.

Example: ABC Bookstore has Php 5,000 in notes payable as of December 31, 2023.

Utilities Payable

These refer to amounts due to providers of water, electricity, telephone, and other basic services.

Example: ABC Bookstore has Php 2,000 in utilities payable as of December 31, 2023.

Mortgage Payable

This pertains to debt secured from a financial institution by mortgage or lien on real estate of the business or its proprietor.

Example: ABC Bookstore has Php 100,000 in mortgage payable as of December 31, 2023.
