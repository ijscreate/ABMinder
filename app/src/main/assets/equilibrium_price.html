Equilibrium Price

An equilibrium price is a fundamental concept in economics that plays a pivotal role in determining market stability and efficiency. It represents the point at which the forces of supply and demand intersect, resulting in an optimal balance between the quantity demanded by consumers and the quantity supplied by producers.

In essence, the equilibrium price acts as a harmonious point of agreement between buyers and sellers, ensuring that the market operates smoothly without experiencing either a surplus or a shortage of goods. This delicate balance is achieved when the price of a product aligns perfectly with the level at which consumers are willing to purchase and producers are willing to supply.

When the market price deviates from the equilibrium price and is set above this level, a surplus of smartphones emerges as consumers are less inclined to purchase at the inflated price. This surplus exerts downward pressure on prices as manufacturers seek to offload excess inventory. Conversely, if the price falls below the equilibrium price, a shortage of smartphones materializes due to heightened consumer demand, prompting manufacturers to increase prices to capitalize on the scarcity.
