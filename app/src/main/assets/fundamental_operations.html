Key Terms

  • Fraction: A number that represents a part of a whole. It is expressed as a ratio of two integers, where the numerator represents the part and the denominator represents the whole.
  • Decimal: A number that represents a part of a whole, using the base-10 number system. It is expressed as a series of digits to the right of a decimal point.
  • Percentage: A number that represents a part of a whole, expressed as a fraction of 100. It is denoted by the symbol %.

Example of a Fraction

Consider a pizza divided into 8 equal slices. If you take 3 of those slices, you have 3/8 of the pizza. This is an example of a fraction, where 3 is the numerator and 8 is the denominator.

Numerator: The number of slices you have (3).
Denominator: The total number of equal slices in the pizza (8).

Example of a Decimal

The decimal equivalent of 3/8 is 0.375. This is because 3 divided by 8 equals 0.375.

Decimal: A representation of the fraction 3/8 in the base-10 number system (0.375).

Example of a Percentage

If a product has a discount of 20%, this is equivalent to expressing the discount as a fraction of 1/5 or a decimal of 0.2. Understanding percentages allows businesses to make pricing decisions and communicate discounts effectively.

Percentage: A representation of the discount as a fraction of 100 (20%).
