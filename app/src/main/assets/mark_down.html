Markdown

What is Markdown?

Markdown is a business math term that refers to a reduction of the original retail sales price in order to increase sales. In other words, it is a process where the price list is permanently changed to a reduced price. Salespersons usually offer commodities at a reduced price so that they can stimulate a commodity's demand.

The markdown amount can be calculated using the following formula:

Markdown Amount = Original Price - Sale Price

The markdown percentage can be calculated using the following formula:

Markdown Percentage = (Markdown Amount / Original Price) * 100%

These formulas help in determining the extent of reduction in price and the percentage of markdown applied.

How does Markdown Work?

When it comes to the security market, there are two types of prices; bid prices and ask prices. Bid prices, in this case, refers to the prices that buyers are offering on securities, while ask prices are prices that the person selling the securities is offering. To get a bid-ask-price, you need to find the difference between the highest and the lowest bidding prices. Note that it is the difference between the dealer's price charges to a retail customer in exchange for security and the inside market price that is referred to as markdown. Markdown in when the difference in price is negative while markup is when the difference in price is positive.

What is Discount?

Discount is a reduction in the price of a product for a specific reason, such as a promotion, a loyalty program, a bulk purchase, or a special occasion. Discount is a temporary price change that is offered to certain customers or for a limited time. Discount is also known as a concession or a rebate.

What is the Difference Between Markdown and Discount?

The main difference between markdown and discount is that markdown is a permanent price change that affects the profitability and inventory value of the product, while discount is a temporary price change that does not affect the profitability and inventory value of the product.

Markdown is a decision made by the retailer to lower the price of a product that is not selling well or is outdated. Markdown is a loss for the retailer, as it reduces the margin and the revenue of the product. Markdown also affects the accounting and reporting of the retailer, as it lowers the value of the inventory and the cost of goods sold.

Discount is a strategy used by the retailer to attract customers, increase sales, and compete with other retailers. Discount is a benefit for the customer, as it lowers the price and increases the value of the product. Discount does not affect the accounting and reporting of the retailer, as it does not change the value of the inventory and the cost of goods sold.

Another difference between markdown and discount is that markdown is applied to the product itself, while discount is applied to the transaction or the customer. Markdown is visible to all customers, as it is shown on the price tag or the shelf. Discount is not visible to all customers, as it is only given at the checkout or with a coupon.

Examples of Markdown and Discount

Suppose a broker desires to increase the volume of his or her stock sales in a stock market. To achieve this, the broker will decide to sell the stocks at a markdown price (reduced price). In other words, the decision of a broker to sell stocks to investors at a lower price than the current selling price in the stock market is referred to as the markdown. For instance, let's say broker X sells shares of ABC stock to buyers at a rate of ₱20 per share. In the broker market, the original price of these shares was ₱40 per share. In this case, the broker will markdown on the shares when he sales will be; ₱20 - ₱40 = - ₱20.

Another example of markdown is when a retailer decides to lower the price of a product that is not selling well or is out of season. For example, a clothing store may markdown the price of winter jackets in spring to clear the inventory and make room for new arrivals. The markdown percentage can be calculated by dividing the difference between the original price and the sale price by the original price. For example, if a jacket was originally priced at ₱100 and is now on sale for ₱70, the markdown percentage is (₱100 - ₱70) / ₱100 = 0.3 or 30%.

A retailer has a winter coat that was originally priced at ₱200. However, after the winter season, the retailer decides to markdown the coat by 50% to clear the inventory. The markdown percentage is calculated by finding 50% of the original price, which is ₱100. So, the markdown amount is ₱100. To get the markdown price, we subtract the markdown amount from the original price: ₱200 - ₱100 = ₱100. This means the coat is now priced at ₱100 after the markdown.

A retailer has a loyalty program that offers 5% off on every purchase for members. A customer buys a shirt that was originally priced at ₱40. To calculate the discount amount, we find 5% of ₱40, which is ₱2. So, the discount amount is ₱2. To get the discount price, we subtract the discount amount from the original price: ₱40 - ₱2 = ₱38. This means the customer pays ₱38 for the shirt after the discount.

A retailer has a buy one get one free promotion on selected items. A customer buys two pairs of socks that were originally priced at ₱10 each. Since it's a buy one get one free offer, the customer pays for only one pair of socks. So, the discount amount is ₱10, which is the price of one pair of socks. The customer effectively gets one pair for free, so the total price paid is ₱10. This means the customer pays ₱10 for two pairs of socks after the discount.

A retailer has a clearance sale that offers an additional 20% off on already marked down items. A customer buys a dress that was originally priced at ₱100, but was marked down by 40% to ₱60. First, we calculate the markdown amount by finding 40% of ₱100, which is ₱40. So, the dress is marked down by ₱40. Then, with the additional 20% off, we find 20% of ₱60, which is ₱12. So, the customer receives an additional discount of ₱12. The final discount price is ₱60 - ₱12 = ₱48. This means the customer pays ₱48 for the dress after both markdown and additional discount.

Types of Markdown

There are two main types of markdown that businesses use to increase sales and customer satisfaction:

  • A sale/event: This type of markdown happens when you place an offer on your sale. For instance, you can have an offer like say Buy 2 Get 2 free, which translates to 50% off. This 50% off is meant to lure customers to buy the commodity, hence increasing your business cash flow.
  • Permanent price Change: This is where the price lists of commodities are permanently changed to a lower price. For instance, let's assume that you have different styles of shoe in 4 colors and selling them at $100 each. After a period of two months, you have managed to sell two of the colors including a few of the third color. However, when it comes to the fourth color, none has been sold. Now, you find yourself in a situation where the customers are declining to buy the fourth color of shoes at $100. You are then forced to lower the price so that you can attract the customers to purchase them.

Why is Markdown Important?

Markdown helps to stimulate the trading activities among customers with believing that the numerous commissions will lead to higher sales which will, in turn, offset the loss incurred in the negative spread. Markdown also helps to clear excess or unwanted inventory and generate cash flow for the business. Markdown can also increase customer loyalty and satisfaction by offering them attractive deals and discounts.
