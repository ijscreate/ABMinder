Operating Activities

Operating activities are the core activities that a business performs to earn revenue. These activities affect the cash flow coming in and out and determine the net income of the business. They are found in the business’ financial statements, particularly the cash flow statement, and are considered the most important section since it provides cash flow and determines the business’ ability to pay its current expenses such as labor costs and debt repayment.

Examples of Operating Activities Resulting in Revenue Generation:

  • Cash receipts from sales
  • Sales of shares
  • Income earned from investment
  • Settlements of lawsuits and insurance claims
  • Accounts receivables collection
  • Supplier refunds

Examples of Operating Activities Resulting in Cash Outflows:

  • Employee payments
  • Supplier payments
  • Tax payments
  • Refunds to customers
  • Settlements of fines and lawsuits
  • Interest to creditors
  • Equipment purchase
  • Interest payment on loans
  • Dividends

Examples of non-cash transactions that are excluded from determining cash flows from operating activities include depreciation, depletion and amortization expense, and gains and losses from the sale of plant assets and the retirement of bonds.

Preparation Methods for Operating Activities Section:

The operating activities section in the statement of cash flows can be prepared using two methods: Indirect Method and Direct Method. Both methods calculate the same total of cash flows from operating activities, but the methodologies are different.

Examples of Operating Activities in a Business:

  • Sales
  • Marketing and Advertising
  • Finance
  • Accounting
  • Customer Service
  • Human Resources
