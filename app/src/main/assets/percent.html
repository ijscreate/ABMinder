Lesson 1.7: Percent

A. Introduction to Percent:

What is Percent?

Percent is a way of expressing a fraction out of 100. It is denoted by the symbol "%".

The word percent means out of each hundred or per one hundred. The word comes from the Latin phrase per centum which translates to "by the hundred." So, for every one hundred units of something, a single percent would represent one out of those hundred units, or one percent.

Percentage Formula:

The percentage formula is given by:

The percentage = (Part / Whole) * 100

where the "Part" represents the portion of the whole that you want to express as a percentage.

Example: 25% represents 25 out of 100.

Key Points:

  • Percent is a ratio per hundred.
  • It is often denoted by the symbol "%".
  • Percentages are used to express proportions, ratios, and comparisons.

B. Converting Between Decimals, Fractions, and Percentages:

Conversions:

Percentages can be converted to decimals and fractions, and vice versa.

Example: Convert 0.75 to a percentage.

0.75 as a percentage: 0.75 × 100 = 75%

When converting decimals to percentages, multiply by 100. To convert percentages to decimals, divide by 100.

Example: Convert 40% to a decimal.

40% as a decimal: 40 ÷ 100 = 0.4

When converting percentages to decimals, divide by 100. To convert decimals to fractions, use the decimal as the numerator and 100 as the denominator.

Example: Convert 0.6 to a fraction.

0.6 as a fraction: 0.6 = 6/10 = 3/5

Key Points:

  • To convert percentages to decimals, divide by 100.
  • To convert decimals to percentages, multiply by 100.
  • Decimals can be converted to fractions by using the decimal as the numerator and 10, 100, 1000, etc., as the denominator.
  • Percentages can be converted to fractions by using the percentage as the numerator and 100 as the denominator.

C. Percentages in Real Life:

Applications:

Percentages are widely used in various real-life scenarios, including finance, statistics, and everyday comparisons.

Example 1: Exam Scores

An exam score of 85% indicates that the student answered 85 out of 100 questions correctly.

Example 2: Discounts and Sales

A store is offering a 20% discount on a product originally priced at $50. Calculate the discounted price.

Original Price: $50

Discount: 20%

Discounted Price: $50 - (20% of $50)

$50 - (0.20 × $50) = $40

The discounted price of the product is $40.

Example 3: Population Growth

The population of a city increased from 500,000 to 600,000 over five years. Calculate the percentage increase.

Initial Population: 500,000

Final Population: 600,000

Percentage Increase: ((Final Population - Initial Population) / Initial Population) × 100

((600,000 - 500,000) / 500,000) × 100 = 20%

The population of the city increased by 20% over five years.

Example 4: Interest Calculation

If you invest $1,000 at an annual interest rate of 5%, calculate the interest earned after one year.

Principal Amount: $1,000

Annual Interest Rate: 5%

Interest Earned: ($1,000 × 5%)

$1,000 × 0.05 = $50

The interest earned after one year is $50.

Example 5: Sales Tax

If an item costs $80 and the sales tax rate is 8%, calculate the total cost including sales tax.

Item Cost: $80

Sales Tax Rate: 8%

Total Cost: $80 + (8% of $80)

$80 + (0.08 × $80) = $86.40

The total cost, including sales tax, is $86.40.

Example 6: Commission Calculation

An employee earns a 15% commission on total sales. If the total sales amount is $2,500, calculate the commission earned.

Total Sales Amount: $2,500

Commission Rate: 15%

Commission Earned: ($2,500 × 15%)

$2,500 × 0.15 = $375

The commission earned is $375.

Understanding percentages is essential for interpreting data, making comparisons, and analyzing information in different fields.
