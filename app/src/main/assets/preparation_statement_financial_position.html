Preparation of Statement of Financial Position

In preparing the SFP of a single proprietorship, the following steps must be applied:

  1. The SFP must have a heading comprising the name of the business, title of the report, and date covered by the report.
  2. Its left margin is usually subdivided into two; extreme margin for major Subheadings (e.g., current assets; plant, property, and equipment; other noncurrent assets; current liabilities; noncurrent liabilities; and owner’s equity) and inner margin for specific account titles.
  3. At the right margin, the extreme money column is also for major subheading while the inner margin is for specific accounts.
  4. A double rule is placed under total assets and total liabilities and owner’s equity. As expressed in the accounting equation, asset must equal liabilities and owner’s equity.

Example of Preparing the SFP of a Single Proprietorship:

The Statement of Financial Position (SFP) summarizes the financial position of a business at a specific point in time, typically the end of a reporting period. It provides insights into the company's assets, liabilities, and owner's equity, presenting a snapshot of what the business owns and owes. The SFP is a crucial financial statement used by investors, creditors, and other stakeholders to assess the financial health and performance of the business.
