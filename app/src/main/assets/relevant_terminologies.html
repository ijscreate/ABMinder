Relevant Terminologies

Share Capital: Share capital is the sum of all funds raised by the company in the form of ordinary shares and preference shares.

Preference Shares: Preference shares confer preferential rights for their holders, such as entitlement to a dividend out of profits before ordinary shareholders.

Redeemable vs. Irredeemable: Preference shares can be of two types: redeemable and irredeemable. Only irredeemable preference shares are treated as share capital.

Authorized Share Capital: It is the maximum amount of share capital that a company is authorized to raise.

Issued Share Capital: Issued share capital is the total value of the shares a company sells.

Subscribed Share Capital: Subscribed share capital is the monetary value of all the shares that the investors have committed to buy.

Paid-up Share Capital: Paid-up share capital is the amount of money a company has received from shareholders in exchange for its shares.

Share Premium: The difference between the par value of a company’s shares and the total amount a company received for shares.

Dividend: It is the distribution of profits to shareholders.

Bonus Shares: These shares are distributed by a company to its current shareholders free of charge.

Right Issue: It is an invitation to existing shareholders to purchase additional new shares in proportion to their shareholding in the company at a discount to the market price.

Retained Earnings: Retained earnings comprise the income that the company retains within the business.

Revaluation Surplus: The result of an upward revaluation of a non-current asset.

Capital Reserves: Statutory reserves which a company is required to set up by law.

Revenue Reserves: Non-statutory reserves consisting of profits which are distributable as dividends at the discretion of the company.

Redemption: The reacquisition of the entity’s own equity instruments.
