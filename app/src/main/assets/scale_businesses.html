Overview of Business Scales in the Philippines

Micro Enterprises:

Definition: Micro enterprises are the backbone of the Philippine economy, representing 91.4% of all establishments. They typically have assets of PHP 3,000,000 or less and employ one to nine individuals.

Local Impact: These small businesses are often family-owned and operated, serving local communities with essential goods and services. They play a vital role in job creation, income generation, and poverty alleviation at the grassroots level.

Small Enterprises:

Definition: Small enterprises account for 8.2% of total establishments in the Philippines. They have assets ranging from PHP 3,000,001 to PHP 15,000,000 and employ between 10 to 99 workers.

Economic Contribution: Small businesses contribute to economic growth by providing employment opportunities, fostering entrepreneurship, and diversifying economic activities across different regions. They are key drivers of innovation and competitiveness in various sectors.

Medium Enterprises:

Definition: Medium enterprises make up 0.4% of total establishments in the country. With assets between PHP 15,000,001 to PHP 100,000,000 and employing 100 to 199 workers, they represent a crucial segment of the business landscape.

Innovation and Growth: Medium enterprises play a significant role in driving innovation, creating value-added products and services, and contributing to the overall economic output of the country. They often serve as a bridge between small businesses and large corporations.

Large Enterprises:

Definition: Large enterprises have assets exceeding PHP 100,000,001 and employ 200 or more individuals.

Economic Influence: Large corporations significantly impact the economy through substantial investments, job creation on a larger scale, market dominance in various industries, and contributions to GDP growth. They often have a significant influence on policy-making and industry trends.

Government Support for MSMEs:

  • The Philippine government recognizes the importance of Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) in economic development and has implemented various programs to support their growth.
  • Legislation such as the Magna Carta for Small Enterprises (Republic Act 8289) aims to provide assistance to MSMEs through access to financing, capacity-building programs, market linkages, and other forms of support.
  • Initiatives like the Barangay MicroBusiness Enterprises (BMBEs) Act promote economic development in rural areas by providing incentives and benefits for small businesses with limited assets.
