Statement of Financial Position

The Statement of Financial Position (SFP) is a financial statement that provides an overview of a company's financial condition as of a specific date. It is also known as a balance sheet and is composed of three main elements: assets, liabilities, and capital.

Assets are resources owned by the company that have value, such as cash, equipment, and property. Liabilities are debts or obligations that the company owes to others, such as accounts payable and notes payable. Capital refers to the investment or equity of the owner in the business.

The SFP is important for the owner of a business because it provides a snapshot of the company's financial health. It shows the value of the company's assets, the amount of its liabilities, and the balance of the owner's interest in the business. This information is useful for making informed decisions about the company's operations and future direction.

Assets on the SFP are classified as either current or non-current. Current assets are those that are expected to be converted to cash or used up within one year or less. Examples of current assets include cash, accounts receivable, and inventory. Non-current assets are those that are not expected to be converted to cash or used up within one year. Examples of non-current assets include property, plant, and equipment.

Liabilities on the SFP are also classified as either current or non-current. Current liabilities are those that are due within one year or less. Examples of current liabilities include accounts payable and notes payable. Non-current liabilities are those that are due after one year. Examples of non-current liabilities include long-term borrowings and deferred tax liabilities.

Capital on the SFP is the residual interest in the assets of the business after all liabilities have been paid. It represents the owner's investment or equity in the business. Capital can be increased through the injection of new funds or through the retention of profits. It can be decreased through the payment of dividends or the repayment of loans.

The SFP is prepared using the accrual basis of accounting, which means that it includes all transactions that have occurred during the accounting period, regardless of whether or not cash has been exchanged. This allows for a more accurate picture of the company's financial position.

The SFP is a financial statement that provides an overview of a company's financial condition as of a specific date. It is composed of assets, liabilities, and capital and is important for the owner of a business because it provides a snapshot of the company's financial health. By understanding the information presented on the SFP, the owner can make informed decisions about the company's operations and future direction.

Here is an example of a statement of financial position:

XYZ Company
Statement of Financial Position
As of December 31, 2022

Assets:

  • Current Assets:
    • Cash ₱100,000
    • Accounts Receivable ₱50,000
    • Inventory ₱75,000
  • Total Current Assets ₱225,000
  • Non-Current Assets:
    • Property, Plant, and Equipment ₱400,000
    • Less Accumulated Depreciation (₱100,000)
    • Net Property, Plant, and Equipment ₱300,000
  • Total Non-Current Assets ₱300,000
  • Total Assets ₱525,000

Liabilities and Equity:

  • Current Liabilities:
    • Accounts Payable ₱50,000
    • Accrued Expenses ₱25,000
  • Total Current Liabilities ₱75,000
  • Non-Current Liabilities:
    • Long-Term Debt ₱150,000
  • Total Non-Current Liabilities ₱150,000
  • Total Liabilities ₱225,000
  • Equity:
    • Common Stock ₱200,000
    • Retained Earnings ₱100,000
  • Total Equity ₱300,000
  • Total Liabilities and Equity ₱525,000

In this example, XYZ Company has total assets of ₱525,000, total liabilities of ₱225,000, and total equity of ₱300,000. The company's assets consist of ₱225,000 in current assets and ₱300,000 in non-current assets. The company's liabilities consist of ₱75,000 in current liabilities and ₱150,000 in non-current liabilities. The company's equity consists of ₱200,000 in common stock and ₱100,000 in retained earnings.

The statement of financial position provides valuable information to stakeholders, including investors, creditors, and management. It allows stakeholders to assess the financial health of the company and make informed decisions about investing in or lending to the company. It also allows management to make strategic decisions about the allocation of resources and the management of risk.
