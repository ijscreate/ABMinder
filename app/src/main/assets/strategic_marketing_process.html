Strategic Marketing Process

1. Objective Setting

Definition: Setting specific, measurable goals that a business aims to achieve within a defined timeframe.

Importance: Provides a clear focus and direction for marketing efforts, ensuring alignment with overall business objectives.

2. Marketing Strategy Development

Definition: Creating a growth strategy to introduce products or solutions to target audiences not currently served.

Significance: Helps organizations expand their market reach, attract new customers, and increase brand visibility and awareness.

3. Strategy Evaluation and Control

Definition: Ongoing process of decision-making, implementation, and assessment of marketing strategies' effectiveness.

Purpose: Enables organizations to monitor performance, identify areas for improvement, and make informed decisions to optimize marketing efforts.

4. Mission Identification

Definition: Defining the organization's purpose, primary customers, products/services, and operational scope.

Role: Guides strategic decision-making, shapes organizational culture, and communicates the company's identity and values to stakeholders.

5. Situation Analysis

Definition: Utilizing methods to analyze internal and external factors affecting the organization's capabilities, customers, and business environment.

Benefits: Provides insights into market trends, competitive landscape, customer needs, and organizational strengths and weaknesses to inform strategic planning.

Example: Situation Analysis

In the Philippines, a tech startup conducts a situation analysis to assess its market position and competitive landscape before launching a new mobile app. By examining internal factors like technological capabilities, team expertise, and financial resources, as well as external factors such as market trends, consumer preferences, and regulatory environment, the startup gains valuable insights.

Through the situation analysis, the startup identifies a growing demand for mobile solutions in the Philippines, particularly in the e-commerce and digital payment sectors. They also recognize the presence of established competitors offering similar apps but lacking certain features that customers desire.

Armed with this information, the startup refines its marketing strategy to emphasize the unique features and benefits of its app, targeting tech-savvy consumers seeking convenience and security in mobile transactions. By leveraging the insights from the situation analysis, the startup can tailor its messaging, pricing, and promotional efforts to effectively position the app in the competitive Philippine market and drive user adoption and engagement.
