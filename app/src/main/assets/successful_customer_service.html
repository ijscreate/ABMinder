Successful Customer Service Strategy in the Philippine Business Enterprise

What is Customer Service?

Customer Service is the act of taking care of the customer’s needs by providing and delivering professional, helpful, high quality service and assistance before, during and after the customer’s requirements are met.

Steps in Developing a Successful Customer Service Strategy

  1. Create a Customer Service Vision

    The first step in creating a customer service strategy is communicating the customer service vision to employees. Employees need to understand what the vision and organizational goals are for customer service and understand their responsibility to help achieve that vision.

  2. Assess Customer Needs

    Organizations can’t meet the needs of their customers without understanding what customers want. A “Customer Needs Assessment” is done by soliciting feedback through focus groups, satisfaction surveys, or customer comment cards, and developing a comprehensive plan to meet and exceed customer expectations.

  3. Hire the Right Employees

    Hiring with the customer in mind is another step in an overall strategy for strong customer service. Employees must be screened properly to ensure that they possess the disposition and skill set to help support a strong customer service environment.

  4. Set Goals for Customer Service

    Once customer needs and expectations are identified and customer satisfaction is measured, it is time to create goals for achieving customer satisfaction.

  5. Train on Service Skills

    If you hire correctly, your employees will have a natural ability to serve your customers well. However, everyone can benefit from practical teaching on the organization’s approach to customer service.

  6. Hold People Accountable

    Employees should have a good understanding of how their service to the customer affects the organization’s overall performance and need to be held accountable for achieving customer satisfaction goals.

  7. Reward and Recognize Good Service

    There should be a well thought out system for acknowledging and rewarding employees for providing great service.

Reasons Why Companies Trust Customer Service Operation to Filipinos

Filipinos are Highly Literate

Filipinos boast a high literacy rate, with a strong emphasis on education in the country. This educational focus equips Filipinos with critical thinking skills, problem-solving abilities, and a capacity to adapt to various situations, making them well-suited for customer service roles that require quick thinking and effective communication.

Filipinos are Proficient in Speaking the English Language

English is widely spoken and understood in the Philippines, being one of the official languages. This proficiency in English enables Filipinos to communicate effectively with a global audience, making them valuable assets in customer service operations that cater to international clients.

Filipinos are Warm and Hospitable

Known for their warmth, friendliness, and hospitality, Filipinos naturally excel in providing excellent customer service. Their innate ability to empathize, listen attentively, and make customers feel valued contributes to creating positive customer experiences and building strong relationships.

Filipinos Embrace Western Culture

The Philippines has a strong affinity for Western culture, which includes familiarity with Western norms, practices, and consumer preferences. This cultural alignment allows Filipinos to understand and relate to the needs and expectations of customers from Western countries, enhancing their ability to deliver tailored and culturally sensitive customer service.

Examples of Successful Customer Service Strategies in the Philippines

Cebu Pacific

Offering a loyalty rewards program called GetGo, where regular customers can earn points for free flights, encouraging customer loyalty and repeat business.

Starbucks

Focusing on meeting customers where they want to be met, utilizing mobile transactions, and emphasizing personalization to enhance the overall customer experience.

Globe Telecom

Investing significantly in capital spending to provide fast internet service to two million homes by 2020, demonstrating a commitment to meeting customer needs and expectations.

BPI (Bank of the Philippine Islands)

Implementing programs like Manny & Me and BPI SHAPE to educate children on saving and provide skills development for senior high school students, showcasing a commitment to financial literacy and community engagement.

Suki System

Creating stable relationships between buyers and sellers through an unwritten contracted network, where buyers receive extended credit terms, high-quality goods, and special treatment in return for regular purchases and recommendations.
