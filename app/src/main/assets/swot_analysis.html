SWOT Analysis Overview

SWOT analysis is a strategic planning tool used to evaluate the Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats of an organization or a project. It is a widely used method to analyze the internal and external factors that can impact a business, project, or venture.

Key Points:

  • SWOT analysis evaluates internal (Strengths and Weaknesses) and external (Opportunities and Threats) factors.
  • Strengths and Weaknesses are internal factors, while Opportunities and Threats are external factors.
  • By analyzing these factors, businesses can identify their competitive advantage, potential areas for improvement, and strategies to capitalize on opportunities or mitigate threats.
  • SWOT analysis is often presented in a 4-boxes layout, with each quadrant representing one of the four factors.
  • To conduct a SWOT analysis, gather a team of experts from different departments and use collaborative brainstorming.
  • Tools like Creately, Visual Paradigm, or Lucidspark can be used to create SWOT analysis models and list out information in bullet points.
  • During brainstorming, add ideas to the relevant quadrant of the grid and highlight important or urgent ones.
  • Use comments to leave feedback and discuss factors further.
  • Once ideas are gathered, prioritize them using an impact/effort matrix and plan out next steps in a Creately action plan template.
