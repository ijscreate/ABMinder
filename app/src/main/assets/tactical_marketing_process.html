Tactical Marketing Process

The tactical marketing process involves several key components that are essential for successful implementation of marketing strategies. Here are the key elements of the tactical marketing process:

  1. Timetable Activity: A tool used to outline a plan of activities and events that will occur during the promotion of a product or service.
  2. Activity Budgets: A detailed roadmap that outlines marketing strategies, tactics, costs, and expected outcomes over a specific period.
  3. Action Plans/Tactics: Translates business goals into marketing objectives, detailing the specific strategies and tactics to achieve those objectives.
  4. Marketing Activities: Activities undertaken by a company to promote the buying or selling of a product or service.
  5. Monitoring and Control: The process of implementing marketing strategies while making necessary adjustments to ensure they stay on track.

Here's an example of the tactical marketing process in action:

Example: GreenTech's Tactical Marketing Plan

Suppose a company named "GreenTech" aims to launch a new line of eco-friendly home appliances in the Philippine market. They have already established their marketing strategy, which includes targeting environmentally conscious consumers and highlighting the unique selling points of their products.

  1. Timetable Activity: GreenTech can create a timeline of events and activities for the next year, including product launches, promotional campaigns, and trade shows.
  2. Activity Budgets: GreenTech can create a detailed budget for their marketing activities, outlining the costs for product development, advertising, events, and personnel.
  3. Action Plans/Tactics: GreenTech can translate their marketing strategy into specific tactics and action plans, such as creating a content marketing plan and participating in trade shows.
  4. Marketing Activities: GreenTech can undertake various marketing activities, including creating educational content, launching a referral program, participating in trade shows, and collaborating with influencers.
  5. Monitoring and Control: GreenTech can monitor the progress of their marketing activities, track their budget, and make adjustments as needed to ensure they achieve their desired results.
