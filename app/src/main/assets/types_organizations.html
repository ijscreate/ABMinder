Types of Organization

As you might have guessed by now, there exist two types of organization:

  • Formal Organization
  • Informal Organization

Formal Organization

In every enterprise, there are certain rules and procedures that establish work relationships among the employees. These facilitate the smooth functioning of the enterprise. Further, they introduce a systematic flow of interactions among the employees. Effectively, all of this is done through a formal organization.

Notably, the management is responsible for designing the formal organization in such a way that it specifies a clear boundary of authority and responsibility. Coupled with systematic coordination among various activities, it ensures the achievement of organizational goals.

Advantages:

  • The formal organization clearly outlines the relationships among employees, making it easier to track responsibilities.
  • An established chain of commands maintains the unity of command.
  • Clear definition of rules and procedures minimizes ambiguity and confusion in individual roles, eliminating duplication of efforts.
  • Stability and predictability in behavior and relationships among members lead to stability in the enterprise.
  • Leads to the achievement of organizational goals and objectives through systematic work cultures and relationships.

Disadvantages:

  • Decision-making is slow due to the need to flow through the respective chain of commands.
  • Rigid nature may lead to low recognition of talent and limited adaptability to change.
  • Does not fully consider the social nature of humans, focusing primarily on structure and work, potentially leading to dissatisfaction among employees.

Informal Organization

Unlike formal organization, informal organization is fluid and there are no written or predefined rules for it. Essentially, it is a complex web of social relationships among members that are born spontaneously. Unlike the formal organization, it cannot be forced or controlled by management.

Advantages:

  • Communication does not need to follow the defined chain, resulting in faster communication.
  • Facilitates socialization within the enterprise, leading to a sense of belongingness and job satisfaction.
  • Allows for true feedback and reactions, covering up various limitations of formal organization.

Disadvantages:

  • Random nature can result in the spread of rumors and chaos within the enterprise.
  • Difficulty in effecting changes and growth without the support of the informal organization, which can work for or against the enterprise.
  • Conforms to group standards and behaviors, which, if against organizational interests, can lead to disruption.
