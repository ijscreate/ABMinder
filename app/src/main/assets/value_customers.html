Value of Customers

All marketing efforts focus on how to create, deliver, and communicate value to the customers. In this section, you’ll learn why customers play such an important role in a business’s marketing activities.

Marketing exists to help organizations understand, reach, and deliver value to their customers. For this reason, the customer is considered the cornerstone of marketing and the foundation of any business' success. One of the primary goals of any marketing strategy should be to identify and meet the needs of the consumer. Considering customer importance at all stages of the marketing process helps build customer satisfaction and increase its long-term goal of repeat business.

Considering customer needs during the development and promotion of a product is not the only way to emphasize customer needs. Customer considerations after the product has been marketed are important as well. Customer service and interaction with the consumer after the product has been sold, not only build strong relationships with the consumer but offer companies valuable information that will help to design more effective marketing efforts in the future.

With this in mind, what is likely to happen when an organization doesn’t understand or pay attention to the customer’s wants?

One of the world’s best-known brands, Coca-Cola, provides a high-profile example of misunderstanding customer “wants.” In an interview, Roberto Goizueta, who was the chairman, director, and chief executive officer of the Coca-Cola Company from August 1980 until his death in October 1997, recounts the disastrous launch of “New Coke” in 1985 and describes the hard lessons they learned when they changed the original taste of Coke. The “New Coke” mission is to increase Coca Cola’s market share against Pepsi and 7 up. However, it created a backlash from lifelong customers, which forced the executives to revert the original recipe and replace the “New Coke” with “Coke Classic”. Marketing Considerations

Some of the considerations to take into account when marketing to your customers are honesty, integrity, and clarity. Keeping what the consumer needs in mind is an integral part of effective marketing. Dishonest advertising campaigns can generate quick sales, but those sales will falter as consumers realize they have been duped. Selling a good product marketed with integrity is the standard. To do this, a company needs to build the customer’s confidence in its product over time, which will keep them coming back and ensures long-term success in the future.

Word-of-Mouth

Underestimating the power of customer’s word-of-mouth is detrimental to your success. Consumers like to talk, whether they are talking about a product they enjoyed or a product that left them wanting for more. Word-of-mouth has a snowball effect, particularly in an age when you can quickly message anyone you know across the globe using your phone. On the other side of the coin, your company can't afford to not consider how quickly its product and reputation can be severely affected by negative reviews. This is why marketing a product honestly and with integrity is important.

The Customer Is Always Right

"The customer is always right" is a motto that exhorts the service staff to give a high priority to customer satisfaction. It was popularized by successful retailers such as Harry Gordon Selfridge, John Wanamaker, and Marshall Field. These men learned early in their careers that the success of their stores depended on the happiness of their customers. They advocated that customer complaints should be treated seriously so that customers do not feel cheated or deceived.

Saying “the customer is always right” can be difficult when a customer has an issue with your organization and truly believes they are “right”. In this situation, they may possibly be making demands that are impossible for you to meet. So, what’s the best way to handle these situations?

A key point to keep in mind is that the customer is always right, in their own mind, although not necessarily in reality. At this stage, it’s critical not to disagree with the customer because that makes them angry and more argumentative. Here are some key points you may want to consider:

  • Don’t focus on the negative.
  • Don’t even say the word, “no.” If you are in a situation where you need to say “no” to a customer, make sure to provide an alternative option for them.
  • Don’t waste time focusing on who’s right or wrong in a situation. Instead, focus on the positive—on what you can do to help the customer.

Reasons Why the Customer is Always Right

  1. The Customers Makes the Business. One reason many businesses still follow “customer is always right” standard is that they know how important their customers are in the long- term success of the business. A business without customers is just a group of people pitching a product to deaf ears. As a business, you want your customers to return as well as feel welcomed and appreciated at the same time. Many businesses lose sight of this fact when they become successful, not realizing that they can still lose everything without the love and loyalty of their customers.
  2. The Backlash Could Be Devastating. The effect of displeasing just one customer can be devastating to any business. Just one “detailed complaint” on social media can have a negative effect on the business’ reputation. This is also true whether you are just a starting company or an established enterprise. When it comes to startups, it’s possible that they may not be able to recover from this damage because new customers depend on positive reviews before trying out a new product or service. Things like this can negatively affect any business in the long run even with the help of a reputation management company.
  3. It’s Good Business Ethics. Business ethics is a group of morally correct actions that further a business’ reputation by treating customers and associates as human beings. Business ethics classes teach that all departments within a business establishment should have a uniform code of conduct. This code of conduct should display the heart of the business, which should include love and loyalty to their customers.
  4. It is set as the Standard of Customer Service Excellence. “The customer is always right” also sets a standard for the customer service department that strives for excellence. Customer service experiences are one of the leading causes of customer loyalty and customer loss. Every customer matters. Therefore, businesses should strive to create an environment of consistent extra-mile service. It is essential that you are communicating effectively so that you and your customers can find the best possible solution to any issue.

Types of Customers

Customers play the most significant part in business. In fact, the customer is the actual boss in a deal and is responsible for the profit for the organization. Customer is the one who uses the products and services and judges the quality of those products and services. Hence, it’s important for an organization to retain customers or make new customers. To manage customers, organizations should follow some sort of approaches like segmentation or division of customers into groups because each customer has to be considered valuable and profitable.

Customers can be of the following types:

  • Loyal Customers: These types of customers promote more sales compared to other customers. Even though they are less in numbers, these customers are the ones who are completely satisfied with the business’ products and services. They revisit the organization over time; hence, it is crucial to interact and keep in touch with them regularly. Loyal customers want individual attention and demand respectful responses from suppliers.
  • Discount Customers: Discount customers are also frequent visitors but they are only a part of business when offered with discounts on regular products and brands. The more is the discount, the more they gravitate towards buying. Focusing on these types of customers is also important as they also generate profits in the business.
  • Impulsive Customers: These types of customers don’t have any specific items on their product list but impulsively buy what they find good or important at that moment. Handling these customers is a challenge as they are not particularly looking for a specific product. They want the supplier to display all the useful products they have so that they can buy whatever they like. If impulsive customers are treated accordingly, there is a high probability that these customers could generate more sales for the business.
  • Need Based Customers: These customers are product specific and only tend to buy items that solve their specific need. These customers should be handled positively by showing them ways to buy products which they may need in the future.
  • Wandering Customers: These are the least profitable customers as sometimes they themselves are not sure what to buy. They are usually new customers and most of the time visit a store just to compare prices. They tend to review the features of the most leading products in the market but usually don’t show interest in buying it. To grab such customers, they should be properly informed about the various positive features of the products so that they develop a sense of interest.

Benefits of Relationship Marketing

  • Cost-effective: Maintaining existing customers through relationship marketing can be highly cost-effective, as it is often less expensive to retain existing customers than to acquire new ones.
  • Increased customer retention: By connecting with customers and providing them with essential information tailored to their individual interests, concerns, pain points, and needs, companies can increase customer retention and loyalty.
  • Improved marketing ROI: With higher customer retention numbers, the costs of customer acquisition decrease, providing companies with a higher budget for building and implementing their relationship marketing strategy.
  • Increased sales: Existing customers often return to make more purchases since they already value the products or services offered by the company.
  • Lead to referrals and higher brand awareness: Happy customers are more likely to share their positive experiences with others, leading to referrals and increased brand awareness.
  • Boost above the competition: Relationship marketing can help companies create loyal customers who stay with them and persuade others to buy from them, giving them a competitive advantage.
